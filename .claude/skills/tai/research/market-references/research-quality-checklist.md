# Market Research Quality Checklist

Verify every item before delivering research output. If any check fails, fix it.

## Sourcing

- [ ] All quantitative claims have a source or are labeled as estimates
- [ ] Sources include URLs, dates, and brief descriptions
- [ ] At least 2-3 sources cross-referenced for critical claims (market size, growth rates)
- [ ] Data older than 18 months is flagged with `[YYYY data — may be outdated]`
- [ ] Funding data verified against Crunchbase, PitchBook, or press releases
- [ ] Pricing data verified against actual pricing pages, not secondhand reports

## Depth

- [ ] Every cell in the depth matrix is filled with specifics (not "good" or "strong")
- [ ] Dollar amounts, percentages, and user counts are included where available
- [ ] Competitor analysis goes beyond feature lists to business model analysis
- [ ] Market sizing shows the math, not just the number
- [ ] "Unknown" is used honestly — never filled with plausible-sounding guesses

## Objectivity

- [ ] At least one contrarian argument per major finding
- [ ] Fact, inference, and recommendation are labeled separately
- [ ] No unacknowledged bias toward the user's idea (validation ≠ cheerleading)
- [ ] Bear case is given genuine effort, not a strawman
- [ ] "Popular" is not treated as "correct" — hype is flagged as hype

## Decision Orientation

- [ ] The output makes a specific decision easier, not just "more informed"
- [ ] Recommendation is concrete and actionable (not "it depends" or "more research needed")
- [ ] Conditions for choosing differently are stated explicitly
- [ ] Next steps are specific actions, not vague suggestions
- [ ] Tradeoffs are specific (not "large market" — say how large, compared to what)

## Completeness

- [ ] Web search was used for real-time data (not just training data)
- [ ] Executive summary captures the headline finding in 3-5 sentences
- [ ] Sources section is present with numbered URLs
- [ ] AskUserQuestion was used to refine direction at least once
- [ ] Depth level matches what was requested (quick/standard/deep)
- [ ] If investor-ready format requested, all investor template sections are filled

## Market-Specific Checks

- [ ] TAM/SAM/SOM math is internally consistent (SOM < SAM < TAM)
- [ ] Growth rates are compared against similar-stage companies
- [ ] Competitor analysis includes at least one "replacement" competitor (manual/DIY)
- [ ] Timing analysis answers "why now?" with specific evidence
- [ ] Moat assessment is honest — "no moat yet" is a valid answer
