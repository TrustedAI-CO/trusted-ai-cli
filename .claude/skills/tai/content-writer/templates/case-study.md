# Template: Case Study

Tell the story of a real problem solved. The reader should see themselves in the
protagonist's situation and want your solution.

## Structure

### Title
Format: "How {who} {achieved result} [with {product}]"
Examples: "How a 3-Person Team Shipped 10x Faster with AI Code Review"

### The Challenge (2-3 paragraphs)
- Who is the protagonist? (role, company size, industry)
- What was the specific problem? (not abstract — concrete pain)
- What had they tried before? Why didn't it work?
- What was at stake? (deadline, cost, quality, team morale)

### The Approach (2-3 paragraphs)
- What did they decide to do?
- Why this approach over alternatives?
- What was the implementation like? (timeline, team size, key decisions)
- Include 1-2 specific technical details or decisions that made the difference

### The Results
Use concrete metrics. Format as a highlight box or bold stats:
- **{Metric 1}:** {number} → {number} ({percentage change})
- **{Metric 2}:** {before} → {after}
- **{Qualitative result}:** {quote or description}

Include at least one quote from a real person involved (or a representative quote
if anonymized).

### Key Takeaways
3-5 bullet points: what the reader can learn from this story and apply to their
own situation. These should be actionable, not just observations.

### Visualization Opportunities
- The Approach: an architecture or flowchart showing the solution design.
- The Results: a before/after comparison diagram if the change is structural.
- Key Takeaways: a decision flowchart if the takeaways involve conditional advice.

## Writing Rules
- Specificity is credibility. "Reduced deploy time from 45 minutes to 3 minutes"
  beats "Significantly reduced deploy time."
- Include honest friction — what was hard, what surprised them, what they'd do
  differently. Unrealistically smooth stories aren't believable.
- The protagonist should be relatable. If the reader can't see themselves in the
  story, the case study fails.
- Numbers first, narrative second. Lead the results section with hard metrics.
